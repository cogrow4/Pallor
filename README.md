# Pallor

A tale of transformation, rejection, and belonging in a world that has forgotten compassion.

## Overview

**Pallor** is a atmospheric/cinematic 2D adventure-platformer where you navigate a dying world as a shapeshifter caught between two forms; human and wolf. Cast out by both species for being neither fully one nor the other, you must survive in a harsh post-apocalyptic landscape and search for the acceptance you've been denied.



## Key Features

### Dual Form Gameplay

- **Wolf Form**: Experience the world in stark monochrome, heightening your senses but limiting your perspective
- **Human Form**: See the world in muted color, revealing hidden details and paths unavailable to the wolf
- Seamlessly transform between forms (with really nice animation) to solve environmental puzzles and navigate treacherous terrain

### A World in Decay

Explore a hauntingly beautiful post-apocalyptic landscape inspired by Isobelle Carmody's *Obernewtyn* and *Rain World*. Every corner tells a story of what was lost, and what struggles to survive.

### Visual Design

Blending the ethereal, hand-drawn artistry of *Silt* with the expressive character design and atmospheric environments of *Hollow Knight*, **Pallor** creates a unique visual experience that shifts with your transformation.

### Emotional Narrative

- Survival through isolation and rejection
- The search for belonging in a world that fears difference
- A story about finding acceptance when both sides have turned their backs
- Discover the one friend who sees beyond the surface

## Story

Born a mutant with the ability to shift between human and wolf, you belong to neither world. The humans chased you out, fearing what they couldn't understand. The wolves rejected you, repulsed by the scent of humanity that clings to your fur. 

Now, alone in a decaying world where civilization has crumbled and nature has turned hostile, you must forge your own path. Every day is a struggle for survival. Every transformation is a reminder of what you've lost.

But somewhere in this broken world, there is hope. Someone who will see you not as monster or aberration, but as friend.
